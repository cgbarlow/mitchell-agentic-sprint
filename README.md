# Mitchell Agentic Sprint

A guided AI-led product that walks an AI-strong builder through six sequential steps to take one of their many ideas and prove out whether it can become a real business.

By the end of the Sprint, the builder has a locked vertical and ICP, 15–30 real buyer interviews with verbatim quotes, an adapted expert framework, a positioning statement, a sales deck, a customer outreach plan, and a pre-seed investor deck — all built from evidence the builder gathered, not generated by AI flattery.

The Sprint does **not** build the product or the website. It gets the builder to the point where they know whether what they're building will sell, and they have the materials to prove it.

## Design constraints (from the concept)

- **Adversarial by default** — every prompt refuses flattery and pushes back on vague answers
- **Behavioral over hypothetical** — interview scripts ban "would you use…" questions
- **Saturation over headcount** — interviews stop when themes saturate, not at an arbitrary count
- **Loopback on validation failure** — failed verticals retain their learning into the next attempt
- **Mini Council** — five skeptical personas (seed VC, churned customer, competitor founder, future-self, grumpy PM) tear up the idea between key steps
- **Output is artifacts, not product** — sales deck, outreach plan, investor deck
